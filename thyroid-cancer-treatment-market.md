<p><p>The thyroid cancer treatment market encompasses several key modalities. Surgery is often the first line of treatment, involving the removal of all or part of the thyroid gland. Iodine therapy uses radioactive iodine to target residual cancerous cells post-surgery. External radiotherapy employs high-energy beams to destroy cancer cells. Thyroxin treatment replaces or supplements hormones for optimal metabolism. Chemotherapy involves using drugs to kill cancer cells, while targeted therapy focuses on specific genetic mutations associated with thyroid cancer. Each treatment type plays a crucial role in managing and combating the disease.</p></p>
