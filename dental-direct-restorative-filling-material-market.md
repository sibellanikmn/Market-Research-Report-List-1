<p><p>The dental direct restorative filling material market encompasses several key types. Composite fillings are made from a resin that blends with the tooth color, offering aesthetic appeal. Amalgam fillings consist of a mix of metals, known for their durability and strength, commonly used in posterior teeth. Glass ionomer fillings release fluoride and bond chemically to the tooth structure, making them suitable for non-load-bearing areas. Other materials may include resin ionomer and compomer fillings, each catering to specific dental needs and preferences.</p></p>
