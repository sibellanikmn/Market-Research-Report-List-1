<p><p>The ion-exchange membrane market is primarily divided into two types: heterogeneous and homogeneous membranes. Heterogeneous membranes feature a distinct structure with varying ionic domains, resulting in improved selectivity and conductivity, making them suitable for various applications like water treatment and energy generation. In contrast, homogeneous membranes possess a uniform structure that ensures consistent ionic transport, often leading to enhanced efficiency and durability. Both types cater to specific industry needs in areas such as desalination, fuel cells, and batteries.</p></p>
