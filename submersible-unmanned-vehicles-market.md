<p><p>The submersible unmanned vehicles market encompasses two primary types: Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) and Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs). ROVs are tethered to a surface vessel and controlled by operators, ideal for tasks requiring real-time human input, such as inspections and repairs. In contrast, AUVs operate independently, following pre-programmed paths for tasks like surveying and data collection. Both vehicle types serve critical roles in underwater exploration, marine research, and various industrial applications, each offering unique operational advantages based on mission requirements.</p></p>
