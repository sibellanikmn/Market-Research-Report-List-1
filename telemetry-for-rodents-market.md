<p><p>Telemetry for rodents involves tracking and monitoring their movements and behaviors using various technologies. GPS telemetry employs Global Positioning System satellites to provide accurate real-time location data, ideal for studying rodents in expansive or challenging terrains. VHF telemetry uses very high frequency radio signals to transmit information from collars or tags, suitable for medium-range tracking in dense environments. Other types may include acoustic or infrared telemetry, which offer alternative methods for capturing data on rodent activity and ecology through different signal technologies.</p></p>
